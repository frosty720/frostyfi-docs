# POL & Protocol Funding

> How FrostyFi funds itself and deepens its own liquidity — from real subscription revenue, without VCs and without dumping tokens.

## What POL is

**POL = Protocol-Owned Liquidity.** Instead of renting liquidity from mercenary farmers who leave the moment incentives stop, FrostyFi's DAO *owns* the PERMA/USDC liquidity outright. That liquidity:

- earns trading fees on every swap (a growing, protocol-owned income stream),
- can't be pulled out from under the market, and
- grows automatically as the product earns revenue.

POL turns the treasury into a productive endowment rather than a balance that only shrinks.

## The flywheel

The core loop ties product revenue directly to the token and the treasury:

```
User subscribes (USDC)
        │
        ├─►  a share buys PERMA on the DEX  ─►  paired into DAO-owned POL
        │                                          │
        │                                          └─►  POL earns trading fees ─► treasury grows
        │
        ├─►  reserves (USDC) back bonds + runway
        │
        └─►  dev funding (continued development)

Users spend PERMA on premium features (native model, agent deploy, hosted endpoints)
        │
        └─►  part burned (supply ↓) · part to lockers (real yield to POLAR holders)
```

Two distinct forces:
- **Buy pressure** — every subscription routes USDC into buying PERMA for POL. Demand scales with real usage, not hype.
- **Utility & yield** — spending PERMA on premium features burns supply and pays POLAR lockers. (See [Tokenomics](/docs/tokenomics#why-you-actually-need-perma).)

<Callout type="info">
The PERMA buy is funded from the gap between what a user *could* spend (their usage cap) and what they *actually* spend on average — so it deepens liquidity without eating into margins.
</Callout>

## Buy from on-chain and off-chain payers

- **Wallet (USDC) subscribers** — the PERMA buy happens in the same flow as the payment, atomically.
- **Card / Coinbase Commerce subscribers** — no on-chain payment, so those are swept into a **batched treasury buyback** on a schedule. Same destination, different path.

A slippage guard protects every buy, and the PERMA/USDC pool is seeded at launch so routine buys don't move the price.

## How the project is funded

FrostyFi is a **sole-operator, bootstrapped** project — no outside capital. Funding happens in two phases:

| Phase | How the dev is paid | Where the surplus goes |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-token** (Phases 1–2) | Fixed salary + running costs, off the top of subscription revenue | Reserve / future POL |
| **Post-token** (Phase 3+) | Same fixed salary off the top — capped and governance-adjustable (POLAR + timelock) | Surplus splits on-chain: majority to POL, plus reserves, buyback & distribute |
| **Once self-funded** (optional, later) | May migrate to a fixed **percentage** of revenue, once a % comfortably exceeds the salary | Unchanged — surplus to POL/buckets |

The principle is the same throughout: **the developer is paid from revenue to keep building, not from a token allocation to sell** — a fixed salary, with everything above it flowing to the protocol. Any token upside the founder gets is bought on the open market with personal capital, exactly like every other holder.

## Why this beats the VC / insider model

- **No overhang.** There is no team or investor bag scheduled to unlock and dump — because none was minted.
- **Aligned incentives.** The dev earns more only when the protocol earns more. To get paid, you keep it alive and growing.
- **Self-deepening liquidity.** Revenue continuously thickens POL, reducing slippage and volatility for everyone.
- **Transparent.** Splits, the dev share, and POL holdings are on-chain and governed by POLAR.

## Roadmap context

The runway math behind this: FrostyFi becomes self-funding at roughly a few hundred paying subscribers, and surplus above the dev's salary + costs flows to POL from there. The token and POL system launch only after the SaaS is proven and self-sustaining — see **[Roadmap & Launch Gates](/docs/roadmap)**.
