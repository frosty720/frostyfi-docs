# Subscriptions & Pricing

FrostyFi has three tiers. Subscriptions are paid in **USDC on Base** through the
`FrostySubscription` smart contract — there's no credit card and no NFT to manage; your
tier is read directly from the chain against your wallet.

## Tiers

See [Models & Usage](/docs/models) for exactly how the monthly budget and the
**≈ calls left** meter work. Model access is binary: Pro and Enterprise both get *every*
paid model — there is no per-model tier gating.

## How payment works

Subscriptions settle in **USDC (6 decimals) on Base**. The app supports two on-chain
purchase flows:

- **Approve + subscribe (2 transactions)** — works for all wallets, including
  smart-contract wallets.
- **`subscribeWithAuthorization` (1 transaction, EIP-3009)** — a gasless-style single
  signature, available for EOA wallets.

To subscribe:

1. Connect your wallet (or use email/social login, which provisions an embedded wallet).
2. Choose **Pro** or **Enterprise** on the pricing page.
3. Approve the USDC spend and confirm.
4. Your tier flips on-chain immediately, and your usage budget is provisioned.

The `FrostySubscription` contract is deployed on Base (mainnet `0x80f6…3716`,
chain id 8453) with a Base Sepolia deployment (`0xF23B…31bd`) for testing. The contract
replaced FrostyFi's earlier NFT/Unlock-based subscriptions.

## Affiliate / referral program

FrostyFi pays a **referral cut on subscriptions** you bring in, settled on-chain:

- Share a link with your wallet as the referrer (`?ref=<your-wallet>`). The referral is
  captured in a cookie that survives the sign-in redirect.
- When someone subscribes through your link, your wallet is passed as the on-chain
  `referrer`, and the contract accrues your share to a claimable **referral balance**.
- Claim your earnings any time from the **Creator Dashboard → Affiliate Earnings** card
  (calls `claimReferral()` on-chain).

The referral cut is set on-chain in basis points (`referralBps`) and is read live in the
UI. The program is designed around a **30%** affiliate share
(`referralBps = 3000`); the contract's shipped default is configurable by the operator.
Self-referrals are ignored.
